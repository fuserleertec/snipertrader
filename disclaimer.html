△General Risk Disclosure

Trading financial markets is inherently speculative and carries a high degree of risk. You should carefully consider whether trading is appropriate for you given your financial situation, trading experience, investment objectives, and risk tolerance. The following risks apply to all forms of financial trading:

  • Market Risk: Prices can move against your position rapidly and without warning due to economic events, geopolitical developments, central bank decisions, or other unpredictable factors.
  • Leverage Risk: Many trading instruments (futures, forex, CFDs) involve leverage, which amplifies both gains and losses. You can lose more than your initial margin deposit.
  • Liquidity Risk: In fast or thin markets, it may be impossible to exit a position at your desired price, resulting in worse execution than anticipated.
  • Volatility Risk: Market volatility can trigger stops, spike through levels, and create conditions where technical analysis performs erratically.
  • Technology Risk: Platform outages, internet connectivity failures, software bugs, or data feed issues can prevent you from executing or managing trades.
  • Psychological Risk: Emotional decision-making, revenge trading, and deviation from your plan are the leading causes of trader failure.

You are solely responsible for all trading decisions you make. SniperTrader accepts no responsibility for any losses incurred as a result of using our products or following any information presented on our platforms.

Different financial instruments carry different risk profiles. USME is designed for use across multiple asset classes. Understand the specific risks of your chosen instrument:

InstrumentRisk LevelKey Risk Factors
Equity Futures (NQ, ES, YM) VERY HIGH High leverage, overnight gaps, margin calls, contract expiry
Forex (EURUSD, GBP/USD) VERY HIGH 24-hour market, political events, central bank intervention
