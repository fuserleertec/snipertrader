The 0–100% scoring system uses seven independently weighted components. The minimum threshold for any master signal is 60% (scoreThresh). Session setups use a lower threshold of 50% (sessionScoreMin) because the session window itself adds contextual weight. Higher scores mean more institutional confluence — and larger position size.

Section 06

The VWAP Suite — Daily Value-Area Framework

The Daily-Anchored VWAP with Standard Deviation Bands is entirely new in the VWAP Suite. It provides a second, independent premium/discount framework — separate from the 50-bar EQ line — based on volume-weighted fair price. Institutions use VWAP as their primary intraday reference; the engine now surfaces this directly on your chart.

VWAP Calculation & Band Construction

The VWAP is calculated from the start of each trading day (newDay reset via GetYYYYMMDD()). It uses the typical price (hlc3) weighted by volume. Standard deviation bands are computed from the cumulative volume-weighted variance:

# Daily-anchored VWAP
rec vwapNum = if newDay then hlc3 * volume else vwapNum[1] + hlc3 * volume;
rec vwapDen = if newDay then volume else vwapDen[1] + volume;
def VWAP_Daily = vwapNum / vwapDen;

# Standard deviation bands
rec vwapVarNum = if newDay then Sqr(hlc3 - VWAP_Daily) * volume else vwapVarNum[1] + Sqr(hlc3 - VWAP_Daily) * volume;
def vwapStdDev = Sqrt(vwapVarNum / vwapDen);
plot VWAP_upper1 = VWAP_Daily + 1.0 * vwapStdDev;
plot VWAP_upper2 = VWAP_Daily + 2.0 * vwapStdDev;
plot VWAP_upper3 = VWAP_Daily + 3.0 * vwapStdDev;

±1σ Bands DARK GREEN / DARK RED · LONG DASH ~68% of volume-weighted range Normal value area. Price oscillates between ±1σ in balanced conditions. Mean-reversion targets.

VWAP + SMC Confluence Framework

The VWAP bands add a second premium/discount layer that works alongside the 50-bar EQ line. The strongest setups occur when both frameworks agree:

✅

Double Discount

Price is below EQ line (discount) AND below VWAP (value discount). BUY signals here have maximum structural support. Institutions are likely accumulating at a value discount.

✅

Double Premium

Price is above EQ line (premium) AND above VWAP (value premium). SELL signals here have maximum structural resistance. Institutions are likely distributing at a value premium.

⚠️

Mixed Signals

Price in discount (EQ) but above VWAP, or in premium (EQ) but below VWAP. The frameworks disagree — reduce size or wait for resolution. One framework is temporarily distorted.

📐

VWAP is dynamic throughout the day. Unlike the EQ line (fixed 50-bar high/low midpoint), VWAP recalculates with every trade. Early in the day, VWAP can shift significantly as volume builds. By midday, it stabilizes. The bands also widen or narrow based on intraday volatility — wider bands = higher dispersion = larger stops may be warranted.

Section 08

Non-Repainting Swing Detector

The VWAP Suite includes a non-repainting swing high/low detector designed to validate structural pivots without the false signals that plague simpler swing indicators. It uses confirmation bars, ATR-based minimum move filters, and reversal detection to identify genuine swing points.

How the Swing Detector Works

01

Pending Swing Detection

A pending swing high is registered when h > Highest(h[1], swingLookback) — the current high exceeds all highs of the last 5 bars. A pending swing low is registered when l < Lowest(l[1], swingLookback). These are pending — not yet confirmed.

02

Confirmation Bar Delay

The detector waits swingConfirmBars (default: 2) bars after a pending signal before evaluating. This is the non-repainting mechanism. On bar N, it evaluates the pending signal from bar N−2. The 2-bar delay ensures the swing is structural, not noise.

03

Fold-Based Validation

During the confirmation window, a fold loop checks that no intervening bar exceeds (for highs) or undercuts (for lows) the candidate pivot. If any bar violates the pivot within the confirmation window, the candidate is invalidated.

04

Minimum Move Filter

A validated swing must also clear the minimum move threshold: minMove = atrSwing × swingATRMult (default: 10-period ATR × 1.0). The swing high must be at least minMove above the prior swing high; the swing low must be at least minMove below the prior swing low.

Swing Reversal Detection
Rev↑ — Bullish Reversal

Fires when swingDir flips from −1 to +1 — a swing low confirms after a series of lower lows, indicating a structural reversal to the upside.

Bubble: "Rev↑" in green below the bar. Also plots a light green horizontal line (revBot) tracking the reversal low. If sellsig == 1 at the time, this confirms the short-momentum period is ending.

Rev↓ — Bearish Reversal

Fires when swingDir flips from +1 to −1 — a swing high confirms after a series of higher highs, indicating a structural reversal to the downside.

Bubble: "Rev↓" in red above the bar. Plots a light red horizontal line (revTop) tracking the reversal high. If buysig == 1 at the time, this confirms the long-momentum period is ending.

Section 10

Live Trade Execution — The Seven-Step Workflow

Every time the USME VWAP Suite generates a signal — master or session setup — run the seven steps below in order. The VWAP bands and session context add two new checks to the standard workflow.

01

Check the HTF Bias Dashboard

Read W:, 4H:, 1H:, HTF:ALIGNED, TREND, and STRUCT labels. If HTF:ALIGNED is green and matches signal direction — full conviction. If HTF:-- — halve position size. Verify TREND:▲/▼ and STRUCT:UP/DN match the signal direction.

02

Check VWAP Context (NEW)

Where is price relative to VWAP? For a BUY: price below VWAP = value discount (ideal). Price above VWAP but in discount (EQ) = mixed — reduce size. For a SELL: price above VWAP = value premium (ideal). Also note which σ band price is near — ±2σ or ±3σ = overextended, expect mean reversion.

03

Identify the ICT Zone

Is an active OB cloud or FVG cloud still visible? Is there an SC bubble confirming the OB origin? Stacked OB + FVG is the most powerful configuration. No zone = unclear structural premise — skip or reduce to minimum size.

04

Read and Grade the Score

For master signals: ≥ 85% = ELITE size · 70–84% = standard · 60–69% = reduced. For session setups: score ≥ 50% is the gate. A session setup with 55% that also has HTF:ALIGNED + VWAP confluence can be treated as a standard-size trade.

05

Identify the Signal Type

Is this a master BUY/SELL, EMA pullback, SB, NYDef, or Asia Breakout? Master signals carry the full SMC stack weight. Session setups carry the time-window + session-extreme weight. A bar that fires both a master BUY and an SB Long is a dual-confirmation — treat as +1 tier.

06

Check SMT, Swing, and SOTD Tier

SMT:BULL on a buy signal = inter-market confirmation. Swing reversal (Rev↑/Rev↓) within the last 5 bars = structural confirmation. SOTD tier: ELITE = full size · PRIME = standard · WATCH = minimum. These are bonus layers — none are required but each adds conviction.

07

Execute and Manage

Entry: signal bar close. Stop: BuyStop/SellStop level (2× ATR). Target: BuyTarget/SellTarget level (3× ATR). During trade: △ Fade = tighten stop. Rev↑/Rev↓ against position = exit immediately. Log: signal type, score %, VWAP position, session, SOTD tier, zone type, SMT, result.
