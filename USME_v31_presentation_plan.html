Module 1 — Overview
What is the Unified Smart Money Engine?
A single ThinkScript study that merges ICT/SMC manual analysis into an automated, scored, multi-tier signal system.
What it is
A multi-layer confluence engine
USME monitors 7 distinct Smart Money conditions simultaneously and converts them into a single numeric score. Signals only fire when enough conditions align — eliminating low-quality setups by design.
What it is not
Not a standalone buy/sell bot
The engine does not place trades. It surfaces confluences, assigns conviction tiers, and alerts. The trader still applies context, selects entries, and manages risk. USME accelerates analysis — it does not replace it.
Core capabilities
BOS / CHoCH detection Multi-TF bias (W / 4H / 1H) FVG with mitigation clouds Order block zones Liquidity sweep detection Killzone gating SMT divergence Smart Money Score 0–100 SOTD 3-tier signal engine ATR stop / target projection

ICT Concepts — Module 3
Market structure: BOS and CHoCH
The engine's structural backbone. Every signal tier requires either a Break of Structure or a Change of Character to have occurred.
Break of Structure (BOS)
Continuation signal
Price closes beyond the swing high (bull) or swing low (bear) of the last bosLen bars, with a candle range exceeding ATR × obDispMult. Represented by green (bull) or red (bear) arrows. The trendState variable carries this forward to all downstream logic.
Change of Character (CHoCH)
Reversal signal — highest weight
A BOS that occurs against the prior trend direction. If trendState was bearish (-1) and a bullBOS fires, that is a CHoCH. Worth 25 points — the single heaviest component in the score. CHoCH is required for a PRIME-tier SOTD.
Sponsoring candle (SC bubble)
On every BOS bar, a small "SC" bubble appears above or below the candle. The sponsoring candle is the displacement bar that drove structure — it often marks where institutional orders were executed, and is the origin candle for any resulting FVG or OB zone.
ICT Concepts — Module 4
Liquidity sweeps & equal highs/lows
ICT theory: institutions need to fill large orders at price levels where retail stops cluster. The engine identifies these sweeps and flags them as potential reversal triggers.
Sweep detection — 3 conditions
Any one of these triggers a sweep
1. Wick exceeds the prior sweepLen-bar swing high/low but closes back inside it.

2. Wick exceeds the previous day's high or low but closes back inside it (PDH/PDL sweep).

3. Single-bar wick above prior bar's high (or below prior low) with close reversal.
Equal highs / equal lows
Liquidity ladder (yellow dots)
Three consecutive bars with highs (or lows) within 2 ticks of each other form a liquidity cluster. These yellow dots flag where buy-side or sell-side stop orders are stacked — prime sweep targets. When price approaches, watch for a sweep + reversal setup.
Signal weighting
A confirmed sweep adds 20 points to the score. A sweep combined with a CHoCH is the classic ICT "sweep and reverse" setup — together they account for 45 of 100 possible score points. The engine requires sweepL (for longs) or sweepH (for shorts) within the confirmation window for a master signal to fire.
ICT Concepts — Module 5
Fair Value Gaps and Order Blocks
Two complementary concepts that identify where price is likely to retrace for institutional order fill. The engine paints them as clouds that disappear on mitigation.
Fair Value Gap (FVG)
Three-candle imbalance
A bull FVG forms when candle[-1]'s low is above candle[-2]'s high — gap of unfilled price. Only valid when the middle candle's range exceeds ATR × fvgDispMult (displacement filter). Green cloud persists until price returns to fill it. The iFVG bubble fires when price enters the zone but closes back above it — a failed fill that inverts the zone into support.
Order Block (OB)
Last opposing candle before BOS
A bull OB looks back obLen bars for a bearish candle (close < open) that preceded a bullBOS. It marks where institutional buy orders were placed. Dark green cloud persists until price trades below the OB low. The "big candle" alternate path fires when a single candle exceeds 1.5× ATR body — flagging an order block from a single aggressive institutional print.
iFVG — inversion fair value gap
When price enters an active FVG zone (touches fvgBullLow) but then closes back above it, the zone has "inverted" — supply has been absorbed and it now acts as demand. The cyan "iFVG" bubble marks this event. It is a high-precision entry trigger when it occurs within the PRIME/ELITE signal context.
ICT Concepts — Module 6
Premium & discount zones
ICT's core principle: buy in discount, sell in premium. The engine quantifies this with a dynamic equilibrium line and shaded clouds.
Equilibrium (EQ line — gray dashed)
50% of the 50-bar range
The midpoint between the 50-bar high and low. Price above EQ = premium territory; price below = discount. Long setups require inDisc (price below EQ). Short setups require inPrem (price above EQ). Displayed on the dashboard as PREMIUM (red) or DISCOUNT (green).
Range clouds
60-bar macro range context
The red cloud above midRange marks the premium zone of the extLen (60-bar) range. The green cloud below marks discount. These provide a broader visual context than the EQ line alone — useful for identifying when price is at an extreme of a larger consolidation range.
How this gates master signals
bullSetup explicitly requires inDisc — the engine will not generate a long master signal if price is in premium. bearSetup requires inPrem. This single rule eliminates the most common retail mistake: buying extended moves into resistance. At PRIME tier, this condition is re-evaluated as part of the full SMC stack.
ICT Concepts — Module 7
Killzones & SMT divergence
Time and correlation filters — two of ICT's most powerful but least-automated concepts. USME implements both.
Session killzones
London 02:00–05:00 · NY 09:30–12:00
ICT identifies these windows as high-probability reversal periods when institutional order flow is at its peak. Vertical dashed lines mark the open of each session. The inKZ flag is used optionally (sotdRequireKZ) to restrict WATCH-tier signals to killzone bars only. The SCORE adds 10 points for any signal during a killzone.
SMT divergence
Correlated pair confirmation
Compares the primary chart to smtSym (default: NQ). A bearish SMT fires when the primary chart makes a new high above PDH but NQ fails to confirm it — institutional distribution in one leg while the correlated instrument diverges. smtBull / smtBear add context to alerts and are optional gates at ELITE tier via sotdRequireSMT.
Multi-timeframe bias stack
HTFtrend tracks 1H swing structure (higher highs = 1). htfBull confirms 4H close direction. wkBull/wkBear captures weekly close direction. All three are labeled on the dashboard. For ELITE signals, both HTFtrend and htfBull must align with the trade direction — this is the hardest filter in the entire engine.

Algorithm Layer — Module 9
How BUY and SELL signals are generated
Two distinct signal paths exist. The master setup path and the EMA pullback path. Both require a score gate — they differ in which ICT conditions they require.
Path A — Master setup (SMC stack)
All 4 conditions within confirmBars (5)
1. Liquidity sweep (sweepL for long / sweepH for short)
2. BOS in trade direction
3. CHoCH in trade direction
4. FVG or OB confirmation
+ Price in discount (long) or premium (short)
+ Score ≥ scoreThresh (default 60)

The 5-bar window means all 4 events must cluster together — spread-out confluences do not fire.
Path B — EMA pullback (momentum)
Trend + pullback + filters
1. EMAs stacked bullish (9 > 14 > 21)
2. Low touches or breaches EMA21 (within 0.2%)
3. Close recovers above EMA21
4. Edge detected (bar first time)
+ Volume OK · RSI 30–70 · MACD bullish
+ Score ≥ scoreThresh − 10

This path fires in trending conditions between structural events — catches continuation moves.
ATR projections on signal bars
On every BUY or SELL bar, two dashed lines plot: Stop at 2× ATR from close, Target at 3× ATR from close. This gives an automatic 1.5R minimum target-to-stop ratio. These are starting points — adjust to local structure (OB/FVG levels, PDH/PDL) before entry.
Algorithm Layer — Module 10
SOTD — Signal of the Day tiers
The SOTD engine runs above the base signal layer. It fires once per day per tier, escalating conviction level as more conditions stack. Think of it as a confidence certificate for the setup.
Watch ◈
Score ≥ 50
BUY or SELL signal fired
trendState aligned to direction
Optional: must be in killzone
Prime ★
Score ≥ 70
Full SMC stack: CHoCH + sweep +
FVG or OB + premium/discount
BUY or SELL signal confirmed
Elite ⚡
Score ≥ 70
All PRIME conditions
1H trend + 4H direction aligned
Optional: SMT divergence confirmed
Once-per-day locking
Each tier fires at most once per day. After firing, the engine ignores further signals of that tier until midnight reset. This prevents alert fatigue from repeating conditions in sideways price action.
Bubble stacking on the chart
WATCH bubble appears 0.5× ATR from the bar. PRIME appears 1.2× ATR. ELITE appears 2.0× ATR. Three separate bubbles can appear on the same bar when all tiers fire simultaneously — the stacked layout makes tier escalation visually clear.
Reading Signals — Module 11
Dashboard label decoder
The label strip across the top of the chart is a real-time state readout. Read it left to right — it tells the full story before you look at a candle.
Trend: ▲ / ▼
EMA 9/14/21 stack direction. Green = bullish stack, red = bearish. Flat dash = mixed.
STRUCT: UP/DN
Last confirmed BOS direction. Persists until a BOS in the opposite direction fires.
1H: BULL / BEAR
1-hour swing structure direction based on HH/LL comparison of hourly bars.
4H: BULL / BEAR
4-hour close direction. Both 1H and 4H must agree for ELITE tier eligibility.
W: BULL / BEAR
Weekly close vs prior weekly close. Macro backdrop — not a signal gate, but informs bias.
RSI: [value]
Current RSI reading. Green <30, red >70. Signals require RSI between 30–70 (not exhausted).
Vol: ✔ / ✘
Volume above 1.5× 20-bar average. Required for EMA pullback path; adds 10 pts to score.
PREMIUM / DISCOUNT
Current price position relative to the 50-bar equilibrium line.
SMT: BULL / BEAR
SMT divergence active. NQ failed to confirm a new high/low on the primary chart.
SCORE: [n]%
Live Smart Money Score. Green ≥ 70, yellow 50–69, gray below 50.
SOTD: tier label
Highest SOTD tier reached today. Persists all day once fired.
HTF: ALIGNED
1H and 4H both agree on direction. Required for ELITE. Green when aligned, gray when split.
Reading Signals — Module 12
Pre-trade signal checklist
Use this decision tree every time a BUY or SELL bubble appears. Work top to bottom — a single red item means you wait.
  • 1
    Score ≥ 60? — Displayed on SCORE label. Below 60 is noise. If EMA pullback path fired, check score ≥ 50 still holds.
  • 2
    Structural alignment? — STRUCT label matches trade direction. A BUY in STRUCT:DN is a counter-trend entry — size down or skip.
  • 3
    HTF agreement? — HTF:ALIGNED label is green, AND 1H and 4H labels match direction. Disagreement between 1H and 4H is a warning.
  • 4
    Price in correct zone? — BUY requires DISCOUNT label (green). SELL requires PREMIUM label (red). Engine enforces this in master path but not EMA path.
  • 5
    Killzone active? — Not mandatory, but LONDON or NY KZ label significantly improves signal quality. Off-session BUY/SELL signals have lower follow-through historically.
  • 6
    FVG or OB cloud nearby? — The ideal entry is a limit order into an active FVG or OB cloud zone, not a market order at the signal bar close.
  • 7
    SMT confirms? — If SMT:BEAR shows on a SELL and NQ diverged, that is high-conviction confirmation. If SMT is neutral, the trade is still valid — just lower conviction.
  • 8
    Stop placement valid? — Engine projects stop at 2× ATR. Check that the auto-stop is beyond the nearest swing point or OB cloud. If not, widen manually.
Reading Signals — Module 13
Alerts, sounds, and workflow
USME fires five alert types with distinct sounds. Knowing what each sound means lets you triage without looking at the screen.
B
Bell — Base BUY signal
Master setup or EMA pullback path fired long. Score appended. Requires immediate chart review.
C
Chimes — Base SELL signal or SOTD PRIME
SELL signal fired, OR a PRIME SOTD trigger occurred. Same sound — check dashboard for SOTD label to distinguish. PRIME alert includes "Full SMC Stack" in message text.
R
Ring — SOTD ELITE
Highest conviction signal of the day. Full SMC stack + HTF aligned. Check SMT and weekly bias in alert message. These are the setups to size into.
W
Bell (soft) — SOTD WATCH
Fires only when WATCH fires but PRIME does not. Score 50–69 range. Use as a pre-alert to get eyes on the chart — not an entry signal by itself.
D
Ding — Momentum fade (△ Fade bubble)
An active EMA pullback signal has lost momentum (EMA9 crossed back below EMA14). If in a trade from that signal, this is a partial-exit or stop-tighten trigger. Not a reversal entry signal.
Recommended workflow
1. Pre-market: note PDH, PDL, weekly bias, daily bias on labels.
2. At killzone open: watch for WATCH or PRIME alert. Get chart on screen.
3. On Bell/Chimes/Ring: read all dashboard labels top-to-bottom. Run the checklist.
4. If 6 of 8 checklist items pass: assess entry into nearest FVG/OB cloud with auto-stop as baseline.
5. On Ding: consider tightening stop or scaling out if in profit.
