04 Weekly Breakdown

Phase-by-Phase Focus Areas

Day 1–2: HTF Calibration
01
Open every session by building the H4/Daily USME map. Mark all active Order Blocks, Fair Value Gaps, and the premium/discount range for the week. No live trades until bias is locked — this is the institutional map that all LTF entries must align with.
USME H4 ScanDaily Bias
Day 3–5: First Live Entries
03
First real executions. London Open only — maximum one entry per session. USME AI Score must clear 70 before trigger. ATR stop below structure, minimum 1:2 RR target. Log every trade in SMC Journal within 1 hour of session close.
London KZScore ≥70Journal
Day 6–8: Dual Killzone Activation
06
Expand to both London and NY Open windows. Maximum 2 trades per day total — not 2 per session. If London produces a winner, NY becomes observation-only. CHoCH + OB confluence is the minimum entry requirement.
Both KZsCHoCH Entry
Day 9–10: Week Review
09
Full TraderEdge Performance OS review. Pull win rate by setup type. Calculate actual vs theoretical RR on each trade. Identify the 1 setup type that performed best — that becomes your primary trigger for Week 3. Complete psych debrief module.
TraderEdgeSMC Journal
Day 11–13: ELITE Score Protocol
11
Raise the entry bar: only setups scoring ≥80 on the USME AI system qualify. This is the discipline pressure test. Many valid-looking setups will be filtered out — that is the point. Quality over activity. Every session that stays flat is a neutral P&L day, not a failure.
USME ≥80ELITE Tier
Day 14: News Navigation Day
14
A scheduled high-impact news day. Practice the full pre-news protocol: flat 2 minutes before, wait for the initial spike and sweep to complete (typically 3–5 minutes post-release), then look for USME CHoCH confirmation on the 5M for a post-news entry if score qualifies.
News ProtocolPost-Event
Day 15: Halfway Milestone
15
Stop. Full system audit. Where are you vs. the profit target? Is daily consistency maintained? No single day over 35% of total gains? Pull TraderEdge sentiment trend for the 15 days. If streak shows degraded discipline, reduce risk to 0.5% for Week 4. If strong, maintain 1%.
TraderEdgeMilestone
Day 16–20: Protect & Extend
16
If you're ahead of target, reduce risk to 0.5% for the remainder of Week 4. The goal now shifts from profit accumulation to capital preservation. Two consecutive losing days triggers an automatic sit-out on Day 3. Never attempt to make up losses — the mathematical edge will recover without forcing it.
0.5% RiskCapital Safe
Day 21–24: Conservative Funded Mode
21
You are in the final stretch. Risk drops to 0.5% absolute maximum per trade. Activate USME's Setup of the Day (SOTD) feature — only PRIME and ELITE tier signals qualify. Skipping a session entirely when no high-quality setup appears is the correct decision.
USME SOTDPRIME/ELITE
Day 25: Week 5 System Review
25
TraderEdge full 25-day performance export. Confirm you have not violated the consistency rule. Calculate remaining buffer to target. If already at target — consider stopping here and entering protection mode for Days 26–29. The challenge is won when the target is locked, not when Day 30 arrives.
TraderEdge★ Checkpoint
Day 26–29: Endgame Protocol
26
Maximum one trade per day. London Open only. USME ELITE score required (≥85). If target has been reached — zero new entries, hold position. The psychological pressure in these final days is the real test. TraderEdge daily check-in is mandatory before any platform is opened.
1 Trade MaxELITE ≥85
Day 30: Challenge Complete
30
Target reached. Challenge evaluation submitted. If all rules were followed, the funded account is yours. Now execute Phase 1 of the USME Master Plan — and begin duplicating the system to Account #2.

06 Milestone Gates

5 Checkpoints That Define the Challenge

At each milestone, trading stops and a full system audit runs. The decision to continue, reduce risk, or pause is made with data — not emotion.

5
Day 5 · Week 1 Milestone
Foundation Verified
Full review of Days 1–5. Are your USME bias maps accurate? Are London Open setups aligning with H4 OBs? Is the pre-session TraderEdge score correlating with trade outcome quality? This milestone gates entry into the dual-killzone execution phase.
  • Pull SMC Journal: check actual RR vs projected on each trade
  • TraderEdge sentiment review: is discipline improving or degrading through the week?
  • USME calibration check: are OBs being marked at correct H4 levels?
  • Pass condition: positive PnL or breakeven — no rule violations in 5 days

10
Day 10 · Week 2 Milestone
Execution Rhythm Established
The midpoint of Week 2. By now you should have 8–10 completed trade journal entries. The data will reveal your highest-performing setup type. That setup becomes the exclusive primary trigger for Week 3's raised score threshold.
  • SMC Journal: identify top-performing setup type by RR and win rate
  • Consistency check: no single day above 35% of total profit — verify now
  • TraderEdge weekly export: generate 10-day performance curve
  • Pass condition: at or above 40% of profit target with no rule violations
Day 10Execution Gate
15
Day 15 · Halfway Milestone
Halfway Audit — The Critical Gate
The most important checkpoint in the entire 30 days. This is where most challenges are won or lost psychologically. The data from Days 1–15 determines your final strategy for Days 16–30. If you're ahead of pace, protect. If you're behind, recalibrate — not force.
  • Full 15-day P&L review — are you ahead, behind, or on pace for target?
  • TraderEdge 15-day sentiment analysis — is your discipline trend positive or degrading?
  • If ahead: reduce risk to 0.5% for Days 16–25, protect the buffer
  • If behind: do NOT increase risk — maintain 1% and let edge play out
  • Identify and eliminate any setup type that is net negative in the journal data
