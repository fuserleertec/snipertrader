Questions

What's the difference between v3.1 and v2? +
v3.1 introduces the SOTD (Signal-of-the-Day) Engine — a real-time scoring system that grades every signal 0–100% and awards ELITE, PRIME, or STANDARD tiers with distinct audio alerts. The stop loss logic moved from 1x to 2x ATR(14) to reduce noise-driven stop-outs. The multi-timeframe bias cascade was also extended to include Weekly structure for long-term directional context.
Why did the stop loss change to 2x ATR? +
It's a deliberate design decision. 1x ATR stops were frequently tagged by normal intraday volatility before the move played out. 2x ATR(14) creates a wider buffer that filters out noise-based stop-outs. The tradeoff is that your 1R risk is larger — you must size accordingly. This is covered in depth in the Module 5 SOTD deep dive.
Do I need prior ICT/SMC knowledge? +
No. Module 1 covers the foundational ICT methodology from scratch — why markets move through accumulation, manipulation, and real delivery. If you already have ICT/SMC experience, you can move quickly through Module 1 and focus on the SOTD-specific modules (3, 5, 8) which contain the most significant changes.
What platform does USME v3.1 run on? +
USME v3.1 is built for ThinkorSwim. You'll need a ThinkorSwim account with at least one chart configured before starting. Installation instructions are included with your subscription access.
What assets can I trade with it? +
USME v3.1 is optimized for NQ (Nasdaq futures), ES (S&P futures), GC (Gold futures), and Forex pairs. The SMT divergence module specifically targets NQ/ES correlation and BTC/ETH for crypto traders. It works on any liquid market with sufficient institutional participation.
Is this a signal service or a learning platform? +
Both — and that distinction matters. The course teaches you to understand every signal the indicator fires so you're never dependent on a black box. You learn the structure behind each label, when to take it, and when to pass. The goal is autonomy, not dependency.
