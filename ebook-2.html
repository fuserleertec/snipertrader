The Market Is Not Random — It's Engineered

Every retail trader is taught the same thing: support and resistance, moving averages, RSI divergence. These aren't bad tools. They're just the wrong side of the trade. The market doesn't move because of supply and demand in the way your first trading book described. It moves because large institutional participants need liquidity — and retail traders, with their predictable stop losses clustered below every obvious level, are the liquidity.

Think about the last time price broke below a key support level, stopped you out, then reversed and ran without you. That wasn't bad luck. That was a buy-side liquidity raid — a deliberate engineered sweep of retail stops. Institutions don't buy at support. They buy after they've swept the retail stops below support, using that wave of sell orders to fill their buy positions at scale.

This is the first and most important mental shift in the ICT methodology: price does not go where demand is strongest. Price goes where liquidity is. Once you see this, you can never unsee it. The rest of this book will show you how to use that knowledge to put yourself on the right side of every move.
