Section 01

What the USME v3.1 Does

The Unified Smart Money Engine (USME) v3.1 merges four institutional detection systems into a single ThinkScript indicator: an Order Flow Detector, a Smart Money Bias Engine, an SMC Toolkit, and the new Signal-of-the-Day (SOTD) Engine. Together they answer one question — is this the moment institutions are moving, and in which direction?

🟩

Order Flow Detector

Tracks Order Blocks, Fair Value Gaps, CHoCH/BOS events, and liquidity sweeps. Paints mitigation clouds that disappear when zones are filled. New: Sponsoring Candle (SC) and Inversion FVG (iFVG) bubbles.

📊

Smart Money Bias Engine

Reads four timeframes simultaneously — 5M/15M, 1H, 4H, and Weekly — to determine directional bias. Outputs STRUCT:UP/DN, 1H/4H/W labels, and the key HTF:ALIGNED confirmation.

🎯

SMC Toolkit + Signals

The output layer. Calculates the 0–100% Smart Money Score from 7 weighted components and renders BUY/SELL bubbles with ATR-based stops and targets. New: △ Fade alerts and EMA pullback signals.

⚡

SOTD Engine (New in v3.1)

Identifies and tiers the day's highest-conviction setup into WATCH, PRIME, or ELITE. Fires distinct alerts (Bell / Chimes / Ring) for each tier. Resets daily. Shows the best setup — not just any setup.

Four Questions the USME Answers
01

Where Is Institutional Structure?

The Order Flow Detector maps every active OB and FVG zone with mitigation clouds. SC bubbles mark the exact BOS bar that created each Order Block. As long as a cloud is visible, the zone is valid and unmitigated.

02

Which Direction Are Institutions Positioned?

The four-tier HTF cascade — Weekly → 4H → 1H → LTF — establishes directional conviction. When the HTF:ALIGNED label is green, institutional money across two primary timeframes agrees on direction.

03

Is the Current Setup High-Enough Quality to Trade?

The 0–100% Smart Money Score answers this. Seven components — CHoCH, Sweep, FVG, OB, Volume, Session, Daily Bias — are weighted and summed. A score below 60% means no signal is generated at all.

04

Is This the Best Setup of the Day?

The SOTD Engine evaluates every signal against three tiers. WATCH fires on a valid signal. PRIME fires when the full SMC stack is present. ELITE fires when HTF alignment is confirmed — the rarest, highest-conviction designation the engine produces.

Section 03

The Smart Money Score System

The v3.1 scoring system is fundamentally redesigned from v2. The old 0–10 point scale is replaced by a 0–100% percentage system made up of seven independently weighted components. The minimum threshold for any signal is 60%. Higher scores mean more institutional confluence — and larger position size.

📌

v2 vs. v3.1: In v2, signals used a 0–10 scale with a threshold of 7.0. In v3.1, the scale is 0–100% with a threshold of 60%. The logic is the same — more confluence = higher score — but the percentage format shows exactly how much of the possible confluence is confirmed.

Section 04

Multi-Timeframe Bias Logic

The USME v3.1 now reads four timeframes — Weekly, 4H, 1H, and the entry TF. The Weekly context is new in v3.1 and sits at the top of the hierarchy. Understanding how the dashboard labels reflect each tier is essential to reading the engine correctly.

The Four-Tier Timeframe Cascade
W

Tier 0 — Weekly: The Macro Blueprint

What it reads: Weekly close direction (wkBull / wkBear — current week vs. prior week close). Dashboard label: W:BULL (green) or W:BEAR (red). Used in the SOTD ELITE tier check — eliteBuyCond requires HTFtrend aligned AND htfBull (4H). Weekly alignment is logged in the ELITE alert message. Does not gate standard signals but adds conviction context.

4H

Tier 1 — 4H / 1H: The Bias Engine (Primary Authority)

What it reads: 4H close direction (htfBull/htfBear) and 1H swing structure (HTFtrend — tracking HH/HL or LH/LL). When both agree, the HTF:ALIGNED label activates in green. This is the most important single label on the dashboard. Green HTF:ALIGNED = institutional consensus across two primary timeframes.

D

Tier 2 — Daily: The Bias Gate

What it reads: Whether current close is above the prior day's high (D:BULL), below the prior day's low (D:BEAR), or inside the prior day's range (D:RANGE). D:RANGE means the daily bias component (+5 points) is not contributing to the score.

5M

Tier 3 — 5M / 15M: The Execution Layer

Where the BUY/SELL bubble appears. The engine monitors liquidity sweeps, OB/FVG zones, CHoCH events, EMA stack alignment, and score ≥ 60% on the entry timeframe. This is where you execute — but only after reading the higher tiers first.

SMT Divergence & Session Filters

Two external confirmation layers elevate a standard signal into a high-conviction trade. SMT Divergence reveals institutional manipulation through inter-market comparison. Session filters ensure the engine rewards signals when institutional desks are most active.

SMT Divergence — Inter-Market Confirmation

Smart Money Technique (SMT) Divergence occurs when two highly correlated assets diverge at a swing extreme. One makes a new high or low; its correlated partner fails to confirm. This is institutional fingerprinting — one instrument was engineered to create a false extreme to collect liquidity, while the other reveals the true direction.

smtBull — Dashboard: SMT:BULL
1

Primary chart (ES) makes new low below prior day's low: l < pdLow

2

SMT symbol (NQ) holds its prior day's low: compL >= pdLow

3

ES sweep was a liquidity hunt. NQ's hold reveals institutional intent is bullish.

smtBear — Dashboard: SMT:BEAR
1

Primary chart (ES) makes new high above prior day's high: h > pdHigh

2

SMT symbol (NQ) fails to confirm: compH <= pdHigh

3

ES breakout was a bull trap. NQ's failure reveals institutional intent is bearish.

⚠️

Never trade SMT as a standalone signal. SMT is a confirmation tool. The full signal stack (OB/FVG + CHoCH + Sweep + Score ≥ 60%) must still be present. In v3.1, SMT is the optional gating condition for the SOTD ELITE tier only.

Section 07

Live Trade Execution — The Six-Step Workflow

Every time the USME generates a signal, run the six steps below in order. The first few times this may take 3–5 minutes. After 50 trades, it will take under 20 seconds. Skipping steps is where most execution errors originate.

01

Check the HTF Bias Dashboard

Read W:, 4H:, 1H:, and HTF:ALIGNED. If HTF:ALIGNED is green and matches signal direction — proceed with full conviction. If HTF:-- — continue only with halved position size. Also verify STRUCT:UP/DN matches the signal direction.

02

Identify the ICT Zone

Look at the signal bar area. Is an active OB cloud or FVG cloud still visible (unmitigated)? Is there an SC bubble confirming the OB origin? Is there a stacked OB + FVG configuration (the most powerful entry type)? A zone must exist — no zone means the structural premise is unclear.

03

Read and Grade the Score

Read the score % from the bubble or the SCORE: dashboard label. Determine your position size before proceeding: ≥ 85% = ELITE size · 70–84% = standard · 60–69% = reduced. Do not change this decision as you continue — anchoring bias will push you to increase size regardless of what follows.

04

Confirm the Session Window

Check the session label: LONDON (2–5 AM) or NY KZ (9:30 AM–12 PM ET)? If yes — killzone +10 is already in the score. If OFF — treat the signal as one grade lower. At 2 PM, a 75% score effectively trades like a 65% in terms of institutional timing. Reduce size by half or skip.

05

Check SMT and SOTD Tier

Read the SMT: label — is SMT:BULL on a buy signal? That's inter-market confirmation. Check for a SOTD tier bubble: ELITE = maximum conviction + full size. PRIME = full SMC stack + standard size. WATCH = valid setup + minimum size. No SMT is fine — it is a bonus, not a requirement.

06

Execute and Manage

Entry: signal bar close (or limit order at OB zone midpoint for a pullback). Stop: BuyStop level on chart (2× ATR). Target: BuyTarget level (3× ATR). During trade: if a △ Fade appears, tighten stop to the midpoint between entry and stop. Log the trade regardless of outcome — score %, SOTD tier, session, zone type, SMT (Y/N), result.

Common Execution Mistakes
🚫

Mistake 1 — Ignoring HTF:ALIGNED: Taking a 75% score signal when HTF:-- shows 1H and 4H disagree. Fix: When HTF:ALIGNED is not green, automatically halve intended position size. If that risk is unacceptable, skip the trade entirely.

🚫

Mistake 2 — Chasing signals mid-bar: Entering before the bar closes because "it looks like a signal is forming." The bar reverses and the signal disappears. Fix: USME is bar-close only. If you missed the close, wait for a retest of the OB or FVG zone and enter on the secondary touch.

🚫

Mistake 3 — Using v2 ATR assumptions: Traders upgrading from v2 assume the stop is 1× ATR. In v3.1, atrStopMult = 2.0. The stop is nearly double at the same position size. Always read the actual BuyStop/SellStop from the chart label and resize accordingly.

🚫

Mistake 4 — Ignoring the session window: Taking a high-score signal at 2:00 PM at full size. An 80% score at 2 PM loses 10 points of context — it is effectively a 70% in terms of institutional timing. Reduce size significantly or skip signals in the afternoon.
